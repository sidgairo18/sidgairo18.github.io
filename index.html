I am an incoming ELLIS PhD Student to be advised by Prof. Bernt Schiele (Max Planck Institute of Informatics, Saarbrücken) and Dr. Francesco Locatello (Amazon Web Services, Tübingen). During the course of my PhD I shall be associated with both the Max Planck Institute for Informatics and Amazon Research. Eventually, I shall be awarded a PhD (in 2025 tentatively) by the University of Saarland.

Previously, I was a Pre-doctoral Research Fellow at Microsoft Research in the Technology for Emerging Markets (now Technology and Empowerment) group, where I worked on applications of Computer Vision and Machine Learning to developing low-cost diagnostic solutions for healthcare. And before that, I worked as a Research Intern at Adobe Inc. in the Media and Data Science Research Group. I also worked as an open source software developer and participated in Google Summer of Code program for 2 years running (2017 and 2018). As a part of GSoC I worked for the organization SCILAB.

What now seems like aeons ago, I completed my Master's and B.Tech with Honours in May, 2020 at IIIT Hyderabad, where I was advised by Prof. PJ Narayanan. My work was mainly on learning robust unsupervised style representations for image regognition and retrieval tasks. I train Deep Neural Networks to solve complex tasks in the areas of Computer Vision, Image Processing and Natural Language Processing.

Research

I am interested in Artificial Intelligence, Computer Vision, Image Processing, Machine Learning and their applications. RespireNet: A Deep Neural Network for Accurately Detecting Abnormal Lung Sounds in Limited Data Setting
Siddhartha Gairola, Francis Tom, Nipun Kwatra and Mohit Jain
IEEE Engineering in Medicine & Biology Society (EMBC), 2021.
code / arXiv / pdf / bibtex

RespireNet is a simple CNN-based model, along with a suite of novel techniques—device specific fine-tuning, concatenation-based augmentation, blank region clipping, and smart padding— enabling one to efficiently use small-sized datasets. We perform extensive evaluation on the ICBHI dataset, and improve upon the state-of-the-art results for 4-class classification by 2.2%
